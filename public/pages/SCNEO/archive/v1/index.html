Risk/Reward's Even Better For Short-Term Bullish Trade But Not Without Volatility

October 16, 2014 8:58am PDT

Dear SCN Elite Opportunity Members,

Although all of the major indices are off on the morning, they've yet to crack yesterday's lows. If that happens, it's likely another sharp move lower heading into tomorrow's options expiration. However, should they hold and find a bid at some point today, without taking out yesterday's low, it may suggest yesterday's sharp capitulation was enough to rally these markets for at least a few days.

I want to quickly point out there is one particular reversal signal I'd really like to see on the NASDAQ 100 (NDX) today to provide further context these markets are done moving lower for a while. I've included an hourly bar chart of the NDX here and as you can see, I've circled yesterday's hourly bars, as well as the first few from today so far.

It's obvious at first glance the NDX is working to put in a bottom as I type. However, before the end of the day, if we could see one of the hours close below the 3X3 DMA (blue line) on this hourly chart here then a subsequent hour close back above it, that would give us even more context and confidence to get long some bullish leveraged index ETF's again, primarily TQQQ, which tracks the NDX, and/or TNA, which tracks the Russell 2000.

The signal has yet to surface and although it's definitely something we'd like to see, there's no guarantee we'll end up getting it. And, if for some reason we don't get it and yesterday's low ends up holding, the major indices still have every right to continue higher now, especially when you consider the breadth of yesterday's capitulatory move to the downside.

Furthermore, even better would be to see this exact same signal surface on the daily chart of the NDX over the next several days, as it relates to the 3X3 DMA on the daily chart. However, there would have to be significantly more volatility to take place in order for that to happen.

Although the volatility is about as high as it's been in years this month, we still haven't managed to achieve the same type of volatility we had back in 2010 or 2011 yet and when you consider just how far these markets have run since then, we have every reason to want to see the volatility at least match that of either May 2010 or August of 2011 before the month ends.

I've included a monthly chart of the VIX here to show you what I'm referring to and although I do think short-term traders too often tend to rely on the VIX on an extremely short-term basis, I think the real value in assessing its movement lies within its weekly and monthly chart, so I do strongly believe the VIX can be a great gauge of fear in terms of suggesting when the longer-term investor should start aggressively stepping back into the markets.

With that being said, this is why I mentioned yesterday it was OK for the longer-term player to start carefully wading back into longer-term positions because assuming we can finish the month of October with a much higher degree of volatility than what we've seen so far, it will strongly suggest a potential bottom that could last for a long while.

This is all obviously very hypothetical leading analysis but that's what you're signed up for here at SCN EO. There's no value in telling you about something after it happens. I strongly believe in the old adage, plan the trade and trade the plan. It requires a tremendous amount of patience and discipline to trade like this but I can assure you, the best traders aren't participating every single day, except for the professional market maker or brokerage firm desk trader.

The bottom line is I suspect if these markets are truly in a longer-term bottoming process right now, we're going to see a further increase in volatility but it's important to remember an increase in volatility doesn't necessarily mean much lower lows.

I should also point out once again TLT, the primary ETF tracking bonds, is down today following yesterday's massive blow off top and equally as important, the Russell 2000 is up, once again leading the markets. I mentioned this in yesterday newsletter edition, suggesting the blow off top in bonds and the fact the Russell 2000 continues to outperform all of the other major indices over the last few days, is most definitely a good sign for equities right now.

Additionally, just because TLT blew off like it did yesterday doesn't mean it can't retrace a good part of that major breakout move before potentially moving lower again. And, when you consider bottoms are usually a process, not an event, then the major indices have every right to make the bulls just nervous enough before they inevitably start gunning for new highs.

Regardless, the risk/reward for stepping back into these markets and starting to nibble for the long-term once again is obviously far better now than it has been for several months.

As for the short-term trader, I want to continue to reiterate the context for getting long some bullish index ETF's is now far more attractive than it has been, especially with yesterday's sharp capitulatory move and I definitely don't think now's the time to be short the major indices, unless of course you've entered into some short positions at much higher levels back when we identified the markets were in trouble.

The bottom line is if your risk tolerance is high, jumping into TQQQ, TNA or SSO right now is worth the downside risk, as long as you are willing to maintain the discipline of cutting the trade on a break of a new low on the NDX, which currently sits just below 3,700.

Now that we've entered the teeth of the new earnings season, it's going to be paramount the overall statistics over the next few weeks favors those big and mega caps either meeting or beating their numbers. More importantly, let's pay attention to any earnings revisions or guidance on a go-forward basis because it could be those companies who move lower on the heels of good numbers that may be an excellent opportunity in the months ahead, assuming of course they don't revise forward guidance lower.

Warmest Regards,

John Monroe and the SCN EO Team
